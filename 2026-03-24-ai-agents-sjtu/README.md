# AI Agents for Research and Engineering: From Automation to Reliable Autonomy

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Venue:** Young Scientist Seminar (Invited Talk), School of Computer Science, SJTU
**Format:** Online (Feishu Meeting)
**Duration:** 18:00–19:00 CST
**Organizer:** Dr. Tao Huang (t.huang@sjtu.edu.cn)

## Abstract

Recent advances in large language models (LLMs) have enabled the emergence of AI agents that can autonomously perform complex research and engineering tasks. From literature review and code generation to experimental design and iterative optimization, these agents are beginning to reshape how scientific discovery and software development are conducted. In this talk, I will present a systematic overview of AI agents for research and engineering, focusing on their capabilities, limitations, and reliability. I will first introduce the core paradigm of agentic systems, including planning, tool use, and self-improvement loops. Then, I will discuss how these agents can be applied to automate research workflows, such as hypothesis generation, experiment iteration, and model development. A key focus of this talk is the challenge of uncertainty estimation and calibration in agentic systems. As agents become more autonomous, understanding and controlling their confidence becomes crucial for safe and trustworthy deployment. I will highlight recent advances in calibration for large models and discuss how these ideas can be extended to agent-level decision making. Finally, I will outline open challenges and future directions toward building reliable, scalable, and trustworthy AI agents for scientific discovery.
